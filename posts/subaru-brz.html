subaru brz: same car as the gt86, different flavour of chaos

✮⋆˙ wait… isn’t this just a gt86?

Yes. And no. The Subaru BRZ and Toyota GT86 are literally twins: same platform, same basic body, same engine, same “let’s make a light, cheap, fun coupe” philosophy. Underneath, they share almost everything important. But on the surface, and in vibes, they split into two different characters.

The BRZ is the Subaru-branded version of the project. Think: same tiny rear-wheel-drive chaos, but with Subaru badges, Subaru marketing, and slightly different suspension tuning depending on model year. It’s the one that makes zero sense if you only know Subaru for all-wheel-drive rally cars — they finally built something RWD and it turned out like this.

Visually, the BRZ gets its own front bumper design, headlight details and wheel styles, plus the default “of course it’s blue” Subaru colour that kind of suits it too well. Parked next to a GT86, it’s like seeing twins with different haircuts and outfits: same bones, different attitude.

✮⋆˙ the same fa20 boxer, but with subaru energy

Under the bonnet, there’s no surprise: it’s still the FA20, a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated flat-four boxer. Subaru provides the engine, Toyota brings fancy fuel injection tech, and together they create an engine that is just powerful enough and way more about revs and response than brute torque.

fa20 in the brz

  • 2.0-liter NA boxer four
  • around 200 hp stock (depending on year/market)
  • loves to rev, doesn’t love being below 3k
  • flat, slightly annoying “torque dip” in the mid-range
  • sounds better with literally any decent exhaust

The engine behaves the same way as in the GT86: it wants you to use gears, plan your overtakes and actually drive the car, not just rely on turbo torque. It’s the kind of engine that makes sense when you’re in the mood, and feels a bit underwhelming when you’re tired and stuck in traffic.

But that’s exactly why enthusiasts like it. There’s something very honest about a car that forces you to participate instead of just pressing the pedal and letting boost do everything.

✮⋆˙ handling: subaru’s version of the same idea

Chassis-wise, the BRZ is still a lightweight, low, rear-wheel drive coupe with the engine mounted nice and low. The balance is the same: pointy front end, playful rear, and a feeling that the car would absolutely love it if you spent every weekend on back roads or at track days.

Depending on year and spec, Subaru tweaked the suspension slightly differently to Toyota. In practice, for most normal humans, the car still feels like what it is: a friendly, talkative, slightly tail-happy coupe that wants to help you learn car control without instantly trying to kill you.

Steering is quick and light but communicative, and the rear end will move if you provoke it — especially on the famously not-amazing stock tyres of early cars. Like the GT86, the BRZ is all about “slow car fast”: you don’t need 300 km/h autobahn runs to have fun. You just need a roundabout and questionable self-control.

✮⋆˙ interior: same layout, subaru badge

Inside, the BRZ is basically the same interior you’ve already seen in the GT86: simple, driver-focused, slightly cheap in places, with the important bits done right. Low seating position, good wheel, nice shifter, clear rev counter, small useless back seats.

The differences are minor — some trims, colours, stitching and little details change by brand and model year — but you still know what you’re sitting in: a not-luxury, not-pretending-to-be-luxury sports coupe.

It’s the kind of interior you feel fine scratching up a bit. It wants helmets, track wristbands, bags full of tools and maybe fast food, not designer handbags and piano black surfaces.

✮⋆˙ gt86 vs brz: which twin is this?

Emotionally, the GT86 reads as “anime protagonist’s car” and the BRZ reads more like “quiet kid in the back of the meet who’s actually fast”. Same underlying car, but:

  • BRZ leans a bit more “subaru nerd” than “toyota marketing nostalgia”
  • default image is blue + Subaru stars, not orange + 86 badge
  • some trims, damping and spec differences depending on year
  • slightly different front bumper and light designs = different face

In practice, you pick the one that matches your personality or the one that was cheaper on the used market that day. They’re more the same than they are different — but car people will absolutely act like it’s a huge personality statement.

✮⋆˙ why the brz is actually a great “first proper car”

As a car model, the BRZ makes so much sense if you:

  • want rear-wheel drive but can’t afford something exotic
  • want to learn track driving or drifting without 400 hp
  • care more about feel than straight-line numbers
  • like modifying cars slowly over time — wheels, suspension, aero, tunes

It’s one of the few modern-ish cars that still feels like a proper enthusiast platform: tons of coilovers, arms, exhausts, tunes, body kits, lips, wings, seats… whatever flavour of chaos you like, the aftermarket has already tried it on a BRZ.

And because it’s a Subaru, there’s that tiny extra layer of nerd cred: people who know will know, and people who don’t will just think it’s a “cool little sports car”.

So yeah, the Subaru BRZ is basically the GT86’s twin. But if the Toyota is the loud golden retriever, the BRZ is the quiet cat that suddenly decides to sprint across the room at 3 a.m. for no reason. Same energy, different brand of feral.
