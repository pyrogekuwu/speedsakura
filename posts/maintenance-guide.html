basic maintenance checklist for people who never opened the hood

if cars make you anxious and the engine bay looks like a metal jungle — this guide is for you. no technical jargon, no guilt, no “my dad says you should know this already.” just the basic things you actually need to check, how often, and what happens if you ignore them.

1. engine oil

how often: every 10–15k km or once a year

why: oil lubricates the engine. low or old oil = engine damage = expensive sadness.

how to check: pull the dipstick, wipe, insert again, check the level between MIN and MAX. color should be brown/amber, not black sludge.

2. coolant (the “don’t overheat” fluid)

how often: glance at it every 1–2 weeks, change every few years

why: prevents overheating. overheating kills engines fast.

how to check: look at the coolant reservoir (never open when hot!). it should sit between MIN and MAX. colors differ by type — pink, green, blue are all normal.

3. brake fluid

how often: every 2 years

why: absorbs moisture over time; old brake fluid = spongy brakes = unsafe.

how to check: small reservoir near the firewall. fluid should be clear yellow, not dark brown.

4. windshield washer fluid

how often: whenever it’s low

why: visibility. also the only fluid you can’t mess up.

how to check: look for a cap with the windshield symbol. fill it until it’s full. done. easy.

5. air filter

how often: once a year or every 15–20k km

why: dirty filter = worse performance + higher fuel usage

how to check: open the airbox, take out the filter. if it looks like a dusty carpet, change it.

6. tires

pressure: check monthly — inside your door frame is a sticker with the correct values

tread: minimum legal is 1.6 mm, but 3 mm is already “change soon”

why: low pressure kills fuel economy + kills tires + makes the car drive like a boat

7. lights

how often: check monthly

why: it’s illegal and dangerous to drive without working lights

how to check: look at reflections, ask a friend, or film the car with your phone while testing each light

8. battery

how often: if your starts feel slower, get it tested

why: weak batteries fail suddenly (usually at the worst moment)

tip: if the battery is older than 5 years, plan to replace it soon.

9. belts

how often: inspect yearly

why: cracked belts can snap and leave you stranded

how to check: look for cracks or fraying. if unsure — just ask a mechanic during oil service.

10. cabin filter

how often: every 1–2 years

why: cleaner air, better heater/AC performance

signs it’s old: foggy windows, dusty smell, weak airflow

what happens if you ignore all of this?

your car will still run… until one day it doesn’t. small maintenance prevents big, expensive breakdowns — it’s literally the cheapest way to keep your car happy.

you don’t need to be a “car person” to do this

you can learn as you go. check one item a week. after a month, you’ll feel like “okay yeah, i get this.” your car will feel better, drive smoother, and stress you out way less.
