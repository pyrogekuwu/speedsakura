how to choose your first car without crying later

your first car sets the tone for your whole “car person” era. choose right and you’ll love it forever. choose wrong and you’ll spend the next 6 months googling “why is my engine making this noise” and messaging mechanics at 2am. here’s the clean, beginner-friendly guide on picking a first car that fits your life, your budget, and your sanity.

step 1: decide what you actually need

not what your pinterest board wants — what *your daily life* needs. ask yourself:

  • city, mixed, or mostly highway?
  • manual or automatic?
  • do you need a backseat or just “vibes”?
  • tiny parking spaces near your home?
  • budget for insurance, fuel and future repairs?

step 2: set a real budget

your budget isn't just “price of the car.” it’s:

  • car cost
  • first maintenance (500–2000 zł)
  • insurance
  • tires if needed
  • new battery maybe
  • unexpected “i didn’t think this would break yet” repairs

step 3: reliability > aesthetic (unfortunately)

cute headlights won’t help you when your alternator dies on a monday morning. look for a car known for:

  • cheap parts
  • simple engines
  • low fuel usage
  • no complicated electronics (90s/2000s ftw)

step 4: red flags on listings

  • “only cosmetic rust” → usually not cosmetic
  • “needs small fixes” → they always mean big fixes
  • no photos of engine bay
  • no interior shots → hiding something
  • price suspiciously low for model/year
  • seller refuses a test drive
  • only night photos (lol run)

step 5: what to check in person

  • cold start — smooth or rattly?
  • smoke from exhaust?
  • weird knocks or vibrations?
  • rust on sills, arches, under trunk mat
  • is oil leaking anywhere?
  • does the clutch bite high (worn)?
  • does it pull to one side (alignment/suspension)?

step 6: take someone who knows cars

seriously. a friend, a mechanic, your uncle who complains about TDI engines. an extra pair of eyes can save you thousands.

step 7: test drive with intention

  • listen for noises when turning or braking
  • check how gears feel
  • does the car idle smoothly?
  • does it overheat sitting still?
  • do electronics all work?

step 8: don’t let emotions win

yes the car is cute. yes the color is perfect. yes it looks adorable with stickers. but if the engine is dying or the frame is rusted in half — don’t do it to yourself. there will always be another cute one.

step 9: when to walk away

  • seller acts weird or avoids questions
  • car “overheats a little”
  • visible structural rust
  • fresh paint on suspicious areas
  • paperwork doesn’t match
  • owner can’t explain maintenance history

step 10: celebrate your first car

once you find the right one, do a small first maintenance, clean it, and enjoy the freedom. everyone starts somewhere — this car is the start of your whole journey.
